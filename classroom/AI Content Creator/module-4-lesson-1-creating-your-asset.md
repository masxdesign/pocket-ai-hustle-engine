# Creating Your Asset Step by Step

## What you'll learn

- A clear, step-by-step process for building your first digital asset from scratch
- How to use the AI workflow you learned in Module 3 in a real build session
- How to keep moving even when you get stuck

## Why it matters

This is the lesson everything has been building toward. You have your idea from Module 2. You have your outline and your strong prompt from Module 3. You have your format and your build plan from the last lesson. Now you open a document and build. Do not skip ahead, do not change your idea, do not research any more tools. Everything you need is already in your hands. This lesson is your build session. Follow each phase in order and you will have a finished draft by the end.

## Explanation

Building your first asset is mostly a matter of sitting down and doing it. But knowing the exact sequence of steps makes it much easier.

Here is the full build sequence:

**Phase 1: Set up (5 minutes)**
Open your AI tool. Open a Google Doc. Give the document a working title. Write one sentence at the top describing what this product is and who it is for. This keeps you focused.

**Phase 2: Generate your outline (10 minutes)**
Use your AI tool to create a structure. Ask it to give you sections, categories, or chapters depending on your product type. Review the outline. Keep what makes sense. Remove or adjust anything that does not fit. You are in control — AI is just suggesting.

**Phase 3: Build the content (30–60 minutes)**
Go section by section. For each section, ask AI to draft the content. Paste each draft into your Google Doc. Do not edit while you are drafting — just get the content in. Editing comes later.

**Phase 4: Review and refine (15–30 minutes)**
Read through the whole document. Edit for clarity and tone. Add your own voice, examples, and experience. Remove anything weak or repetitive. The goal is a clean, useful document — not a long one.

**Phase 5: Format and export (10 minutes)**
Apply simple formatting. A title, section headings, bullet points where needed. Export as PDF. Done.

## Step-by-step

1. Open Google Docs and create a new document
2. Write your one-sentence product description at the top
3. Use AI to generate an outline and paste it into the doc
4. Work through each section — draft with AI, paste, move on
5. Once all sections are drafted, go back to the top and start editing
6. Format the document cleanly (no fancy design needed)
7. Export as PDF

## Example

Build session for a prompt pack:

- 5 minutes: Opened doc, wrote title "30 Prompts for Freelancers Using ChatGPT," wrote one-sentence description
- 10 minutes: Asked AI for 6 categories of prompts. Got: client emails, project proposals, invoicing, content creation, research, and pricing
- 40 minutes: Asked AI for 5 prompts per category. Pasted all 30 into the doc. Added a one-line description to each prompt.
- 15 minutes: Edited prompts, rewrote three that were too vague, added an intro paragraph
- 5 minutes: Added headings, cleaned up formatting, downloaded as PDF

Total: 75 minutes. Product complete.

## Action task

Schedule a 90-minute build session this week. Block it in your calendar. During that session, follow the five phases above.

Do not aim for perfect. Aim for done. A finished, imperfect asset is infinitely more useful than a perfect one that does not exist.

When the session ends, write down where you got to. The next lesson is specifically about pushing through to completion — because most people get 70% done and stall. It will get you over the finish line.

## Common mistake

Editing while drafting. When you stop to perfect every sentence as you write it, you slow to a crawl and often give up before finishing. Separate the drafting from the editing. Draft everything first, then go back and improve.
