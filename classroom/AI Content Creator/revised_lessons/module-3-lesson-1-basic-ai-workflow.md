# The Basic AI Workflow

## What you'll learn

- A five-step workflow that takes any idea from concept to finished product
- How to use this workflow for any type of digital asset
- Why having a repeatable process removes overwhelm

## Why it matters

By the end of Module 2 you had two things: a confirmed idea and a rough outline. You may not have realised it at the time, but you already completed Steps 1 and 2 of the workflow you are about to learn. This lesson names the full five-step process, shows you where you are inside it right now, and maps out exactly what happens next until your product is finished.

## Explanation

The basic AI workflow is five steps:

**Step 1 — Idea**
Define exactly what you are building, who it is for, and what problem it solves. Do this before you open any tool.

**Step 2 — Outline**
Use AI to create a structure for your product. An outline gives you a map to follow. You will not get lost if you have a map.

**Step 3 — Draft**
Use AI to write the first version of each section. This does not need to be good. It just needs to exist. A draft you can edit is infinitely more useful than a blank page.

**Step 4 — Refine**
Read through the draft. Edit it. Add your voice, your examples, and your experience. Remove anything that does not add value. Make it sound like you, not like a robot.

**Step 5 — Publish**
Turn it into a shareable format (PDF, Google Doc, etc.) and put it somewhere people can access it. Done.

That is the entire workflow. Idea, outline, draft, refine, publish.

You will use this for every asset you create. It gets faster with practice.

## Step-by-step

1. Before opening any AI tool, write one sentence describing what you are building
2. Open your AI tool and ask it to create an outline for your product
3. Review the outline and adjust it to match your vision
4. Ask AI to write the content for each section one at a time
5. Edit each section to add your voice and remove fluff
6. Combine everything into a final document and save it as a PDF

## Example

Student wanted to build a prompt pack for people new to ChatGPT.

- Idea: "A pack of 15 beginner prompts for people who just signed up for ChatGPT"
- Outline prompt: "Give me 5 categories of prompts a complete beginner would find useful in their first week using ChatGPT"
- Draft: AI generated 3 prompts per category — 15 prompts total
- Refine: Student edited the prompts, added descriptions, and removed two that were too technical
- Publish: Saved as PDF, uploaded to Gumroad, shared in a beginner Facebook group

Total time: 90 minutes from blank page to published product.

## Common mistake

Skipping the outline step and going straight to drafting. Without a structure, AI will produce a messy, unfocused draft that is hard to edit. A five-minute outline saves you an hour of rewriting. Always outline first.
