# How to Choose Your First Project

## What you'll learn

- A simple three-question framework for choosing your first asset
- How to avoid the "what if I choose the wrong thing" trap
- How to commit to one idea and move forward

## Why it matters

The biggest sticking point for beginners is choosing what to build. They have too many ideas, or they feel like their idea is not good enough, or they are afraid of picking the wrong thing. This lesson gives you a clear process so you can pick something and start building today.

## Explanation

Choosing your first project is not a permanent decision. It is a starting point.

You do not need to find the perfect idea. You need to find a good-enough idea and build it. What you learn from building it will show you what to build next.

Use this three-question framework to filter your ideas:

**Question 1: Can I create a basic version of this in one day?**
If the answer is no, the idea is too big for your first project. Shrink it down or choose something simpler.

**Question 2: Does this solve one specific problem for one type of person?**
Broad ideas are hard to execute. Specific ideas are easy to build and easy to share. "Prompts for freelancers" is more focused than "AI prompts."

**Question 3: Would I find this useful myself?**
If you would find it useful, others like you probably would too. Build for someone who is one or two steps behind where you are now.

If you can answer yes to all three, build it.

## Step-by-step

1. Write down every idea you have been considering — take five minutes and list them all
2. Apply the three-question test to each one
3. Cross out anything you cannot build in one day
4. Cross out anything too broad to solve one specific problem
5. From what remains, pick the one that excites you most and write it at the top of a new page

## Example

A career switcher had four ideas:

1. A course on switching careers with AI — crossed out (too big for day one)
2. A general guide to using AI — crossed out (too broad)
3. A resume template for people switching from corporate to freelance — kept
4. A prompt pack for writing LinkedIn posts during a career change — kept

She picked the prompt pack because she could build it in a few hours and it was based on her own recent experience. She built it that evening.

## Action task

Take your list of ideas from the previous lessons. Apply the three-question test to each one.

Circle the one you will build.

Write it in this format: "I am going to build a [type of asset] that helps [type of person] [solve specific problem]."

Example: "I am going to build a prompt pack that helps freelancers write better client proposals using AI."

## Common mistake

Spending more than 30 minutes choosing. The choice is not the important part. The building is. Set a timer for 20 minutes. By the time it goes off, commit to one idea. You can change direction after you have built something — not before.
