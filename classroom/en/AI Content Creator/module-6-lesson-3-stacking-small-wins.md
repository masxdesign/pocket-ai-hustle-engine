# Stacking Small Wins

## What you'll learn

- What a small win is and why it matters more than one big breakthrough
- How to recognise and record your wins so they build momentum
- How small wins compound into large results over time

## Why it matters

Most people measure progress against a distant goal and feel like they are failing the whole time. Stacking small wins is a different approach. It means noticing and building on every step forward, no matter how small. This keeps you moving when the big result is still far away.

## Explanation

A small win is any action that moves you forward.

Examples:
- You finished the outline for your next product
- You published something for the first time
- Someone downloaded your free resource
- You got your first piece of feedback
- You made your first $1 online
- You updated a product based on feedback
- You shared in a community for the first time

None of these are life-changing on their own. But each one builds evidence that you are a person who does this — who builds, publishes, and shows up.

That evidence changes how you see yourself. And how you see yourself determines how consistently you act.

The stacking effect works like this:

- Small win creates positive feeling
- Positive feeling increases motivation to take the next step
- Next step creates another small win
- The cycle repeats and accelerates

The key is making the wins visible. They are easy to overlook when you are focused on how far you still have to go.

## Step-by-step

1. Create a simple "wins log" — a note in your phone, a Google Doc, a notebook
2. Every time you take an action toward building, write it down
3. Review your wins log at the end of each week
4. At the end of each month, write a short summary of everything you did
5. On days when motivation is low, open the wins log before opening anything else

## Example

A student kept a weekly wins log for three months. When she reviewed it at the end of month three, she found:

- 3 products built and published
- 2 products updated based on feedback
- 140 total downloads
- 12 people who had messaged her with thanks or questions
- $67 in early sales

None of that felt significant week to week. Looking at it as a block, it was real progress. She had built something from nothing in 90 days.

The wins log showed her what was actually happening, rather than letting her feel like she was not making progress.

## Action task

Start your wins log today. Write down every action you have already taken in this course:

- Lessons completed
- Ideas chosen
- Drafts started
- Products published
- Things shared

That is your starting total. Add to it every time you take another step.

## Common mistake

Dismissing small wins as "not counting." Every download counts. Every piece of feedback counts. Every product published counts. The people who build the fastest are the ones who treat every forward step as meaningful, not the ones who only celebrate the big moments.
