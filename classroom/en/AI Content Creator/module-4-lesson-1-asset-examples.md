# Asset Examples: Prompt Pack, Guide, Checklist, Mini Ebook

## What you'll learn

- What each of the four main beginner assets looks like in practice
- Exactly what to include in each type
- Which format suits which kind of idea

## Why it matters

Your toolkit is set. Your idea is confirmed. Your outline and prompt are ready. Before you open a blank document and start building, this lesson shows you exactly what a finished product looks like in each format. Knowing what done looks like makes building it ten times easier. By the end of this lesson you will have confirmed your format and written your structure — then the next lesson is your build session.

## Explanation

**Prompt Pack**

A collection of ready-to-use AI prompts organised around a specific topic or audience.

What to include:
- A short intro explaining who the pack is for and how to use it
- Prompts grouped into clear categories (3–6 categories works well)
- 3–10 prompts per category
- A brief note with each prompt explaining what it does or when to use it

Length: 5–15 pages. Can be shorter. Longer is not better.

---

**Guide**

A short how-to document that walks someone through solving one specific problem.

What to include:
- An intro that states the problem and what the reader will learn
- 3–7 clearly numbered steps or sections
- One or two examples per section
- A summary or next steps section at the end

Length: 5–20 pages. Focus on clarity. Cut anything that is not directly useful.

---

**Checklist**

A practical list of actions, steps, or items related to completing a task.

What to include:
- A title that states the specific task or goal
- Steps organised in logical order
- A brief explanation of each step (optional but adds value)
- A completion note or what to do next

Length: 1–3 pages. Checklists should be concise. If it cannot fit on a few pages, it is a guide, not a checklist.

---

**Mini Ebook**

A short, structured document that teaches one topic in more depth than a guide.

What to include:
- An intro chapter that sets up the problem and the solution
- 3–5 main chapters, each covering one key point
- Practical examples and action steps in each chapter
- A conclusion that ties it together and points to next steps

Length: 15–40 pages. Mini means focused. Do not pad it with filler content.

## Example

Two students. Same topic (AI for beginners). Different formats.

Student A made a checklist: "10 Things to Do in Your First Week Using ChatGPT." One page. Clear steps. Direct value.

Student B made a prompt pack: "50 Prompts for Complete Beginners." Five categories, ten prompts each, short description for every prompt.

Both are valid products. Both were built in under two hours. Both addressed the same audience. The format just depends on what serves the reader best.

## Action task

Look at the format breakdown above and confirm which type you are building.

Write a simple structure for your product right now:

- If building a prompt pack: list your 4–6 categories
- If building a guide: list your 4–7 steps or sections
- If building a checklist: list your 8–12 checklist items
- If building a mini ebook: list your 3–5 chapter titles

This is your build plan. Save it alongside your outline from Module 2. In the next lesson you will open a document, use everything you have prepared, and build the actual product. Everything up to this point has been preparation. The next lesson is where you build it.

## Common mistake

Mixing formats. Some beginners try to make a checklist that is also a guide that is also a prompt pack. Pick one format. Build it cleanly. You can create a second product in a different format once the first one is done.
