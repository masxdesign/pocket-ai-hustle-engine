# First 24-Hour Quick Start Plan

You do not need a week to get started. You need 24 hours and a clear sequence of steps.

This plan assumes you have no prior experience building digital products. Follow it in order. Do not skip ahead.

---

## Hour 0–1: Get Clear

**What to do:**

1. Open a blank document or notebook
2. Write down one type of person you could help (example: beginners learning ChatGPT, freelancers, career changers)
3. Write down one problem that type of person commonly has
4. Write down one simple product you could create to address that problem (checklist, prompt pack, guide, or template)

Do not spend more than 60 minutes on this. If you have more than one idea, pick the simplest one and move on.

**Your output:** One sentence describing what you will build and who it is for.

Example: "I will build a prompt pack for people who are new to ChatGPT and do not know where to start."

---

## Hour 1–3: Generate and Outline

**What to do:**

1. Open your AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or similar)
2. Use this prompt:

"I want to create a [type of product] for [your audience]. The goal is to help them [solve the problem]. Give me a clear outline with [4–6 sections or categories]. Keep it simple and beginner-friendly."

3. Review the outline. Remove anything that does not fit. Adjust the structure until it feels right.
4. Paste the final outline into a new Google Doc. This is your build document.

**Your output:** A structured outline for your product in a Google Doc.

---

## Hour 3–6: Draft the Content

**What to do:**

1. Go through each section of your outline one at a time
2. For each section, use this prompt:

"Write the content for [section name] from the outline above. Keep it practical and beginner-friendly. Avoid jargon. Aim for [length — example: 150 words or 5 prompts]."

3. Paste each section into your Google Doc as you go
4. Do not edit yet. Just get the content in.

**Your output:** A complete first draft in your Google Doc.

---

## Hour 6–8: Edit and Refine

**What to do:**

1. Read through the full draft from top to bottom
2. Edit for clarity — remove anything confusing or unnecessary
3. Add your own voice, examples, or context where it helps
4. Check that each section delivers on what the outline promised

You do not need to rewrite everything. Focus on fixing what is unclear and adding what is missing.

**Your output:** An edited, readable draft.

---

## Hour 8–9: Format and Export

**What to do:**

1. Add a clear title at the top of the document
2. Apply simple formatting: headings for each section, bullet points where appropriate
3. Add a short intro at the top (two to three sentences describing what this is and who it is for)
4. Export as PDF (File > Download > PDF Document in Google Docs)

No design needed. Clean and readable is enough.

**Your output:** A finished PDF ready to share.

---

## Hour 9–10: Publish and Share

**What to do:**

1. Upload the PDF to Gumroad, Payhip, or a Google Drive link — whichever is easiest for you right now
2. Write a short sharing post using this template:

"I just created [name of product] for [type of person]. It covers [main benefit]. I built it because [honest reason]. It is [free / $X]. Happy to answer any questions."

3. Share it in one relevant community — a Facebook group, a Reddit thread, a Discord server, or your own social media

**Your output:** A published product that real people can access.

---

## What Comes Next

You have now done what most people only talk about.

Over the next 48 hours:
- Monitor for downloads, responses, or questions
- Reply to anyone who engages and ask: "What was most useful? What was missing?"
- Write down any feedback you receive

In the next 30 days:
- Update the product based on feedback
- Build your second product using the same process
- Keep your wins log updated

That is the system. You just ran it once. Now run it again.
