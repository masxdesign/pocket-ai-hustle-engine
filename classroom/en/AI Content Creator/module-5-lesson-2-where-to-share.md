# Where to Share Your Work

## What you'll learn

- The best platforms for sharing your first digital asset
- How to choose the right community without spreading yourself too thin
- A simple sharing template you can use immediately

## Why it matters

You have published your product. Now you need to put it in front of people. But sharing feels awkward, especially for the first time. This lesson removes the guesswork and gives you a clear, low-pressure approach to getting your work seen.

## Explanation

You do not need an audience to share your first product. You need to find communities where your ideal reader already spends time.

Here are the best options for beginners:

**Facebook Groups**
Search for groups related to your topic. Freelancers, side hustlers, AI beginners, career changers — there are large, active groups in every niche. Many allow value posts where you share resources.

**Reddit**
Find the relevant subreddit. Look for communities where people ask the kind of questions your product answers. Read the rules before posting. Many communities welcome useful, free resources.

**Discord Servers**
Many niche communities have Discord servers. Join a few in your topic area. Contribute to conversations first, then share your resource when it is relevant.

**X (Twitter/formerly Twitter)**
Write a short post explaining what you built, who it is for, and why you built it. Authentic posts about real work do well. You do not need followers — one share from the right person can reach thousands.

**LinkedIn**
Strong for professional topics. A post about something you built performs well on LinkedIn if it is practical and honest.

Start with one platform. Share in one or two communities. See what response you get. Do not try to post everywhere at once.

## Step-by-step

1. Choose one platform where your ideal reader is likely to hang out
2. Find two or three relevant communities or groups on that platform
3. Spend a few minutes reading recent posts so you understand the community vibe
4. Write a short sharing post using the template below
5. Post it and respond to any comments or questions

**Sharing template:**

"I just created [name of product] for [target audience]. It covers [main benefit]. I built it because I [personal reason — struggle you had, question you kept seeing, etc.]. It is [free / available for $X]. Happy to answer any questions."

Keep it honest and human. Do not oversell. Let the product speak for itself.

## Example

"I just created a 15-prompt pack for people who are brand new to ChatGPT. It covers writing emails, generating content ideas, and summarising long documents. I built it because I spent my first week with ChatGPT not knowing where to start. It is free — link in the comments."

This post was shared in a beginner AI Facebook group. It received 34 comments and 180 downloads in 48 hours.

No fancy copywriting. No audience. Just a useful thing shared honestly.

## Action task

Identify one community you will share your product in. It does not have to be large. It just needs to have people who would find your product useful.

Write your sharing post now using the template above. Have it ready to paste.

Then share it. Today.

## Common mistake

Waiting to build an audience before sharing. You do not need an audience. Audiences are built by consistently sharing useful things. Your first post in a community is the beginning of that, not something that requires you to already have one.
