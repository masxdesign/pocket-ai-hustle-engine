# The 60-Minute Digital Asset Challenge

Build and publish a real digital asset in 60 minutes.

This is not a drill. By the end of this challenge, you will have something real that you can share today.

---

## Before You Start

You need:
- One AI tool (ChatGPT or Claude — free tier is fine)
- Google Docs (free)
- 60 minutes with no interruptions

Set a timer. Let us go.

---

## Minutes 0–5: Choose Your Asset

Pick one of the following. Do not overthink it. Just pick the one that feels most natural.

**Option A — Prompt Pack**
A list of 10–15 prompts on one specific topic.

**Option B — Checklist**
A list of 8–12 steps for completing one specific task.

**Option C — Resource List**
A curated list of 10–15 tools, links, or tips on one specific topic.

Write down:
- Which format you chose
- What topic or audience it is for
- What problem it solves

Example: "Prompt pack for beginners learning ChatGPT. Helps them get started without feeling lost."

---

## Minutes 5–10: Generate Your Outline

Open your AI tool. Use this prompt:

"I want to create a [format you chose] for [your audience]. The topic is [your topic]. Give me [3–5 categories or sections] that this should cover. Keep it simple and practical."

Review the output. Pick the three to five sections that make the most sense. Paste them into a new Google Doc as headings.

---

## Minutes 10–35: Build the Content

Work through each section. For each one, use this prompt in your AI tool:

"For the section called [section name], give me [3–5 prompts / 3–4 checklist items / 3–4 resources]. Make each one clear and immediately useful for a beginner."

Paste each result into your Google Doc under the right heading.

Do not edit yet. Speed over quality at this stage.

Move through all sections without stopping.

---

## Minutes 35–45: Edit

Read through your full document. Make quick fixes:

- Remove anything that is confusing or weak
- Rewrite any item that is too vague
- Add a two-sentence introduction at the top explaining what this is and who it is for
- Add a closing line: "If you found this useful, share it with someone who would benefit."

You are not rewriting everything. You are doing one fast pass for clarity.

---

## Minutes 45–50: Format and Export

Quick formatting in Google Docs:

- Title at the top (large, bold)
- Section headings (use Heading 2 style)
- Clean bullet points for items
- No images needed, no colour needed

Export as PDF: File > Download > PDF Document

Done. You have a real digital product.

---

## Minutes 50–60: Publish and Share

Two options depending on your situation:

**Option A — Share for free**
Upload to Google Drive. Set sharing to "Anyone with the link." Copy the link.

Write this post and share it in one relevant community:

"Just made a quick [format] on [topic] for [audience]. Free to download — [link]. Let me know if it is useful."

**Option B — Sell it**
Create a free Gumroad account. Upload the PDF. Set a price ($5–$15 is appropriate for a quick beginner resource). Copy the product link.

Share the same post with your Gumroad link.

---

## After the Challenge

Your 60-minute asset is live.

Now do three things in the next 24 hours:

1. Reply to anyone who responds and ask: "What was most useful?"
2. Write one thing you would improve in version 2
3. Note the time it took and what you would do differently next time

You have proven that building is possible. The next one will take 45 minutes. The one after that, 30.

This is how momentum starts.
