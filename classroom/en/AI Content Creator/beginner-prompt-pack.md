# Beginner Prompt Pack

A collection of ready-to-use prompts for building digital assets with AI.

Copy any prompt directly into ChatGPT, Claude, or your AI tool of choice. Adjust the parts in brackets to match your specific situation.

---

## Section 1: Idea Generation

Use these prompts when you need help deciding what to build.

---

**Prompt 1 — General product ideas**

"I want to create a simple digital product for [your target audience — example: beginners using AI, freelance writers, career changers]. Give me 10 specific product ideas I could create in one day. For each idea, write one sentence describing who it helps and what problem it solves."

---

**Prompt 2 — Finding your audience's problems**

"What are the 8 most common struggles that [your target audience] face when [specific situation — example: starting to use AI tools, transitioning to freelance, building an online business]? List each problem in one sentence."

---

**Prompt 3 — Product idea from a problem**

"I want to help [type of person] with this problem: [describe the problem]. What are three simple digital products I could create to solve this? For each one, suggest a format (checklist, guide, prompt pack, or template) and explain why it fits."

---

**Prompt 4 — Validating your idea**

"I am thinking about creating a [type of product] for [audience] that helps them [solve specific problem]. Does this seem like something people would find useful? What might make it more valuable? What might make it less appealing?"

---

## Section 2: Outlining

Use these prompts to create a structure before you start writing.

---

**Prompt 5 — Outline for a guide**

"I want to write a short beginner guide on [topic] for [audience]. Create a clear outline with 5–7 sections. Each section should cover one key point and move the reader logically from the problem to the solution."

---

**Prompt 6 — Outline for a prompt pack**

"I am creating a prompt pack on [topic] for [audience]. Suggest 5–6 categories of prompts that would be most useful to them. For each category, write a one-sentence description of what those prompts will help the reader do."

---

**Prompt 7 — Outline for a checklist**

"Create an outline for a practical checklist that helps [audience] complete [specific task]. Include 8–12 items. Organise them in the order they should be completed. Keep each item action-focused and clear."

---

**Prompt 8 — Outline for a mini ebook**

"I want to create a short ebook (15–25 pages) that teaches [audience] how to [achieve specific outcome]. Give me a chapter outline with 4–6 chapters. For each chapter, write a one-sentence description of what the reader will learn."

---

## Section 3: Drafting

Use these prompts to write the content for each section of your product.

---

**Prompt 9 — Drafting a guide section**

"Write the content for the section called [section name] from my guide outline. The guide is for [audience] and the goal is to help them [outcome]. Keep the language simple and beginner-friendly. Aim for around [150–300] words. Use short paragraphs."

---

**Prompt 10 — Writing prompts for a prompt pack**

"Write 5 useful AI prompts for the category called [category name]. These prompts are for [audience]. Each prompt should be practical and immediately usable. After each prompt, write one sentence explaining what it is for and when to use it."

---

**Prompt 11 — Writing checklist items**

"Write 5 checklist items for the section called [section name]. These are for [audience] completing [specific task]. Each item should be an action (start with a verb). Add a one-line explanation under each item to clarify why it matters."

---

**Prompt 12 — Writing an introduction**

"Write a short introduction (100–150 words) for my [type of product] called [product title]. The product is for [audience]. It helps them [solve specific problem]. The tone should be practical and direct. Start by describing the problem, then briefly explain what this product gives them."

---

## Section 4: Editing and Refining

Use these prompts to improve your draft once it exists.

---

**Prompt 13 — Simplifying language**

"Rewrite the following section in simpler language. The reader is a complete beginner with no technical background. Remove any jargon. Use short sentences. Keep the meaning exactly the same. Here is the text: [paste your text]"

---

**Prompt 14 — Making it more actionable**

"Read the following section and suggest how to make it more actionable. I want the reader to know exactly what to do after reading it. Add specific steps or examples where helpful. Here is the text: [paste your text]"

---

**Prompt 15 — Cutting unnecessary content**

"Review the following text and identify anything that is repetitive, vague, or adds no value. Suggest what to cut and why. Here is the text: [paste your text]"

---

**Prompt 16 — Writing a title**

"Suggest 5 clear, specific titles for a [type of product] that helps [audience] with [problem]. Each title should make the benefit obvious. Avoid vague or generic titles."

---

## Section 5: Publishing and Sharing

Use these prompts to write your sharing posts and product descriptions.

---

**Prompt 17 — Product description for Gumroad or Payhip**

"Write a short product description (100–150 words) for a [type of product] called [title]. It is for [audience] and helps them [specific outcome]. The price is [$X]. The tone should be direct and honest. Do not oversell. Focus on what the reader gets and why it is useful."

---

**Prompt 18 — Social sharing post**

"Write a short post I can share in a [Facebook group / Reddit / LinkedIn] to let people know about my new [type of product] called [title]. The product is for [audience] and helps them [solve specific problem]. Keep it honest and human. Under 100 words. No hype."

---

**Prompt 19 — Feedback request message**

"Write a short message I can send to someone who downloaded my [type of product]. I want to thank them and ask one question to get useful feedback. Keep it friendly and under 50 words."

---

**Prompt 20 — Follow-up post after sharing**

"I shared a [type of product] in a community and got some response. Write a short follow-up post (under 80 words) I can share two weeks later. I want to mention that I have updated it based on feedback and invite new people to download it."

---

## How to Use This Prompt Pack

1. Read through all five sections before your next build session
2. Copy the prompts most relevant to where you are in your process
3. Paste them into your AI tool and adjust the bracketed sections
4. Follow up with the AI if the first output is not quite right — prompt in the same conversation
5. Build one product from start to finish using only the prompts in this pack

Every prompt here has been designed for beginners. No technical knowledge required. Just copy, adjust, and use.
