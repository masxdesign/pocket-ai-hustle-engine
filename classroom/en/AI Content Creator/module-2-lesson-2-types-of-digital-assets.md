# Types of Beginner-Friendly Digital Assets

## What you'll learn

- The five most beginner-friendly types of digital assets
- What makes each one easy to create quickly
- Which types work well with AI assistance

## Why it matters

One of the biggest reasons people do not start is because they cannot picture what they are supposed to build. This lesson makes it concrete. By the end, you will have a clear menu of options to choose from.

## Explanation

There are five types of digital assets that beginners can realistically create in a day or less.

**1. Checklists**
A list of steps, items, or actions related to a specific task. Easy to create. Easy to use. High perceived value when they save someone time.

Examples: "Pre-launch checklist for your first product," "Daily AI workflow checklist," "10-step content creation checklist"

**2. Prompt Packs**
A collection of AI prompts organised around a topic or goal. Extremely popular right now. Easy to create. Useful for anyone using AI tools.

Examples: "50 ChatGPT prompts for freelancers," "30 prompts for creating digital products," "Instagram caption prompts for coaches"

**3. Templates**
A pre-built framework someone can copy and customise. Could be a Notion template, Google Doc template, content calendar, or email sequence template.

Examples: "Weekly content planning template," "Client onboarding email template," "Simple business plan template"

**4. Mini Guides**
A short PDF (5–20 pages) that walks someone through solving one specific problem. Not a full course. Not a book. Just one problem, solved clearly.

Examples: "How to set up your first Gumroad store," "A beginner's guide to freelancing with AI," "How to write a sales page in one hour"

**5. Resource Lists**
A curated list of tools, links, communities, or resources on a topic. People pay for curation because finding the best resources takes time.

Examples: "50 free AI tools for creators," "Best communities for freelance writers," "Free stock photo sites that actually have good images"

## Step-by-step

1. Read through the five types above
2. Think about which type feels most natural for you to create
3. Think about which type matches the knowledge or skill you identified in the last lesson
4. Narrow down to two or three options
5. Move to the next lesson to decide which one to build first

## Example

A freelance writer who recently started using AI created a prompt pack called "30 ChatGPT Prompts for Freelance Writers." She listed prompts for writing emails to clients, generating article outlines, creating social media bios, and handling invoicing.

She created it in a Google Doc, converted to PDF, and sold it for $9 on Gumroad. It required no technical skills and zero design experience.

## Action task

From the five types above, pick two that feel like a good fit for you. Write them down.

Next to each one, write one specific idea you could create in that format. Just one sentence per idea is enough.

Example: "Prompt pack — 20 prompts for people new to ChatGPT"

## Common mistake

Trying to combine multiple types into one mega-product before you have built anything. Start with the simplest version of one type. You can always add more later. A 10-item checklist that exists is worth more than a 100-page guide that never gets finished.
